Mounting Card

How to mount the deck camera on the RAM Tough-Claw and confirm it has power. No tools beyond your hands are required.

What you're installing: a weatherproof camera and control box, held by a RAM® Tough-Claw™ clamp mount. The clamp does the gripping — the two ball-joint arms below it do the aiming.

1

Open the claw

Back off the two wing knobs on the Tough-Claw base until the jaws open wide enough to fit around the rail.

2

Clamp it to the rail

Pick a straight, solid section with a clear view of what you want recorded — avoid rail that flexes, gets stepped on, or is used for tie-off.

Reference photo — wide shot of the mounted unit

Close the jaws around the rail and hand-tighten both wing knobs as tight as you can get them. Push and pull on the mount — it should not move or twist at all.

Reference photo — Tough-Claw closed on the rail, close-up

Check it again after day oneVibration can work a fresh clamp loose. Re-check both wing knobs after the unit's first day underway.

3

Aim the camera

Loosen the ball joints on the two swivel arms just enough to move them, fine-tune the lens angle, then tighten both joints firmly so the camera can't sag or spin loose.

Reference photo — camera & enclosure on the double-arm
4

Route the cable

Keep the cable clear of doors, ladders, and mooring lines, and secure any slack.

5

Power up & confirm

Flip the toggle switch on the power pigtail — it comes out the bottom of the box — to ON.

Reference photo — toggle switch on the power pigtail, set to ON

Then check the button on the bottom of the box: once the unit has power, the ring around it glows green. That's the only check you need — the camera connects and starts recording on its own.

Button ring lit GREEN  =  unit is powered
Reference photo — close-up of the green-lit button, underside of the box

Leave the button aloneIt's for service use only and isn't part of setup — no need to press it.

Green light not on? Check the power connection first. Still out — contact IT: esmith@marquettetrans.com

Status Light Reference

What the small red light on the camera enclosure is telling you, and what to do about it.

Button ring — green

Bottom of the box. Green means the unit has power — see page 1 for setup.

Status light — red

Top of the enclosure, by the Wi-Fi antenna. Reports system health — this page. Hard to see in direct sun; cup a hand over it.

Pattern What it means What to do
Solid — steady on
 NormalRecording, camera reachable, storage OK, temperature normal. No action needed.
Slow, even blink (~1s)
 DegradedCamera and recording are fine, but the unit has no vessel Wi-Fi/network connection. No urgent action. Contact IT if it stays this way for more than a day.
2 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — camera unreachableThe control box can't reach the camera itself. Contact IT.
3 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — recording stalledCamera is reachable, but the video isn't writing correctly. Contact IT.
4 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — storage offlineNothing is being recorded right now. Contact IT right away.
5 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — running hotThe camera electronics or enclosure are over temperature. Check nothing is blocking airflow or the box isn't in direct sun, then contact IT.

A brief double- or triple-flash right before the unit reboots or shuts down (for example, right after someone presses the button) is normal and isn't a fault — it settles once the unit finishes restarting.

Not sure what you're seeing, or a fault won't clear? Contact IT: esmith@marquettetrans.com
