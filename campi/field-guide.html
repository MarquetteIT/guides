Mounting Card

How to mount the deck camera on the RAM Tough-Claw and confirm it has power. No tools beyond your hands are required.

What you're installing: a weatherproof camera and control box, held by a RAM® Tough-Claw™ clamp mount. The clamp does the gripping — the two ball-joint arms below it do the aiming.

1

Open the claw

Loosen the two wing knobs on the Tough-Claw base until the jaws open wide enough to fit around the rail.

2

Clamp it to the rail

Pick a straight, solid section of rail with a clear view of what you want recorded — avoid rail that flexes, gets stepped on, or is used for tie-off. Close the jaws around it and hand-tighten both wing knobs as tight as you can. Push and pull on the mount afterward — it should not move or twist at all.

Check it again after day oneRe-check both wing knobs after the unit's first day underway — vibration can loosen a fresh clamp.

3

Aim the camera

Loosen the ball joints on the two swivel arms, point the lens where you want it, then tighten both joints firmly so it can't sag or spin loose.

4

Route the cable

Keep the cable clear of doors, ladders, and mooring lines, and secure any slack.

5

Power up & confirm

Flip the toggle switch on the power pigtail at the bottom of the box — to ON ( | ). Then check the button, also on the bottom: the ring around it glows green once powered. That's the only check you need — the camera connects and starts recording on its own.

Button ring lit GREEN  =  unit is powered

Leave the button aloneIt's for service use only and isn't part of setup — no need to press it.

6

Powering OFF?

Press and hold

for 3-4 seconds. Red indicator light on top will flash and will go off once it shuts down cleanly — do that before disconnecting power for storage or maintenance

Green light not on? Check the power connection first. Still out — contact IT: esmith@marquettetrans.com

Status Light Reference

What the small red light on the camera enclosure is telling you, and what to do about it.

Button ring — green

Bottom of the box. Green means the unit has power — see page 1 for setup.

Status light — red

Top of the enclosure, by the Wi-Fi antenna. Reports system health — this page. Hard to see in direct sun; cup a hand over it.

Pattern What it means What to do
Solid — steady on
 NormalRecording, camera reachable, storage OK, temperature normal. No action needed.
Slow, even blink (~1s)
 DegradedCamera and recording are fine, but the unit has no vessel Wi-Fi/network connection. Try one button press (clean reboot, not the pigtail switch — see note). Contact IT if it stays this way for more than a day.
2 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — camera unreachableThe control box can't reach the camera itself. Try one button press (clean reboot, not the pigtail switch — see note). Contact IT if it doesn't clear.
3 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — recording stalledCamera is reachable, but the video isn't writing correctly. Try one button press (clean reboot, not the pigtail switch — see note). Contact IT if it doesn't clear.
4 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — storage offlineNothing is being recorded right now. Contact IT right away.
5 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — running hotThe camera electronics or enclosure are over temperature. Check nothing is blocking airflow or the box isn't in direct sun, then contact IT.

Use the button, not the pigtail switch, for reboot or shutdown. The switch cuts power instantly and can corrupt the current recording. One press reboots cleanly; holding it ~3 seconds shuts it down cleanly — do that before disconnecting power for storage or maintenance. Press it once, or hold for shutdown: two quick presses wipes the saved Wi-Fi and switches to a local hotspot instead.

A brief double- or triple-flash right before the unit reboots or shuts down (for example, right after someone presses the button) is normal and isn't a fault — it settles once the unit finishes restarting.

Not sure what you're seeing, or a fault won't clear? Contact IT: esmith@marquettetrans.com
