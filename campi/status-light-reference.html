Status Light
Reference

What the small red light on the camera enclosure is telling you, and what to do about it.

Button ring — green

Around the push-button on the bottom of the box. Green means the unit has power. That's all it tells you — see the mounting card for setup.

Status light — red

On top of the enclosure, inline with the small Wi-Fi dome antenna. This is the one on this card — it reports the camera system's health. In direct sunlight it can be hard to see — cup a hand over it to check.

Pattern What it means What to do
Solid — steady on
 NormalRecording, camera reachable, storage OK, temperature normal. No action needed.
Slow, even blink (~1s)
 DegradedCamera and recording are fine, but the unit has no vessel Wi-Fi/network connection. Try a single press of the button for a clean reboot (see note below) — not the pigtail switch. Contact IT if it stays this way for more than a day.
2 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — camera unreachableThe control box can't reach the camera itself. Try a single press of the button for a clean reboot (see note below) — not the pigtail switch. Contact IT if it doesn't clear.
3 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — recording stalledCamera is reachable, but the video isn't writing correctly. Try a single press of the button for a clean reboot (see note below) — not the pigtail switch. Contact IT if it doesn't clear.
4 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — storage offlineNothing is being recorded right now. Contact IT right away.
5 quick flashes, pause, repeat
 Fault — running hotThe camera electronics or enclosure are over temperature. Check nothing is blocking airflow or the box isn't in direct sun, then contact IT.

Use the button, not the pigtail switch, for reboot or shutdown. The switch cuts power instantly and can corrupt whatever's currently recording. One press reboots the unit cleanly; holding it about 3 seconds shuts it down cleanly — do that before disconnecting power for storage or maintenance. Press it only once, or hold for a shutdown: two quick presses wipes the saved Wi-Fi network and switches to a local hotspot instead.

A brief double- or triple-flash right before the unit reboots or shuts down (for example, right after someone presses the button) is normal and isn't a fault — it settles once the unit finishes restarting.

Not sure what you're seeing, or a fault won't clear? Contact IT: esmith@marquettetrans.com
